## Abstract

Despite significant research on happiness, its relation to health conditions contains gaps. Yet understanding how happiness is related to health conditions is critical to improving healthcare and improving quality of life for those with medical conditions.

This study aimed to examine whether a significant health condition, defined as an impairment that substantially impedes an individual's ability to perform daily tasks, was correlated with happiness levels, measured on a scale from 1 to 7. Data was collected from 755 individuals through a Google Form and analyzed using a chi-square test for independence. The test resulted in a p-value of 0.43959 and showed no correlation between happiness and health.

This invites a re-examination of factors for happiness and suggests resilience may help those with health conditions adapt so they have a similar scale of happiness to those without health conditions. Future research should focus on resilience, specifically hedonic adaptation, and its role in those with health conditions.
